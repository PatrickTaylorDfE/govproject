School information glossary

Descriptions of what each piece of data we ask for means.

Admission appeals – spring only

Only schools responsible for their own appeals enter this information

Recent academy converters complete this if they converted before spring census day – Thursday 19 January 2020.

We only ask for information on appeals:

  • from people about not being offered the place they wanted for the start of the 2019 to 2020 academic year
  • that are the responsibility of the school’s governing body

If you have contracted another agency, such as your local authority, to handle appeals, and you governance code is ‘VA’, ‘FO’ or ‘CA’, you should still complete the module.

Where your school handles appeals jointly with other schools, your return should only include appeals for your school.

We do not need information on:

  • appeals submitted after Saturday 31 August 2019
  • in-year appeals – appeals against a refusal to admit a pupil during the 2019 to 2020 academic year

Primary admissions appeals

Primary and middle-deemed primary schools should include all appeals.

All through schools must provide information here on all their admissions appeals that relate to entry into years reception to 6 inclusive.

Please note: infant admissions appeals data are also contained within the primary admissions appeals data.

Infant admission appeals

These are included in primary appeals

We define infant as ‘NC year Actual’ equals ‘Reception’, ‘1’ and ‘2’ (inclusive).

All-through schools must complete this if they teach one or more of these years.

Primary and all-through schools include their admission appeals figures for infants both in the infant and in the primary admissions appeals modules.

Secondary admissions appeals

Secondary and middle-deemed secondary schools give all of their appeals information here.

All-through schools must provide information here on all of their admissions appeals about entry into years 7 upwards.

Appeals about sixth forms and 14 to 19 schools are included in with secondary appeals, so appeals for year 12 and above are include.

Admissions appeals figures to be returned

The number of appeals:

  • lodged
  • withdrawn
  • heard
  • rejected
  • decided in favour of the parents

Admission appeals lodged

Total number of admission appeals lodged by parents between March 2019 and August 2019 inclusive against non-admission to the school for the start of the academic year beginning September 2019.

Appeals lodged as ‘refusal to admit for academic year 2019/20’ are included if the appeal was lodged before the 1 September 2019. This includes:

  • appeals lodged before 1 September, where the hearing or decision took place after that date
  • appeals not pursued to the hearing stage, and those not resolved
  • appeals that did not reach a hearing because a place became available, at your school or another, which was accepted by parents

Admission appeals heard by independent admission appeals panel

Total number of admission appeals heard by an independent appeals panel.

Admission appeals heard by independent admission appeals panel – decided in the parent’s favour

Total number of admission appeals heard and then decided in the parent’s favour by an independent appeals panel.

Admission appeals heard by independent admission appeals panel – rejected

Total number of admission appeals that have been heard and rejected by an independent appeals panel.

Admission appeals withdrawn

Total number of admission appeals withdrawn by parents before reaching an independent appeals panel. This includes appeals that did not reach a hearing because a place became available and was accepted by the parents.

Pupil reconciliation – spring only

Reconciliation is to check that the number of registered pupils you entered matches up with the number of pupils you entered as taught in classes (after allowing for the fact that some registered pupils may not have been in class at the selected time).

The software will ask you to enter the number of:

  • pupils engaged in private study at the selected time
  • pupils attending another educational provision at the selected time
  • pupils attending FE college (if you have any)
  • pupils attending work experience (if you have any)

The software will then execute the reconciliation and generate an error message if pupil numbers do not match.

The reconciliation checks that the number of pupils on roll, on census day that you have given individual pupil data for (not including any guest pupils); matches the sum of the number of:

  • pupils from this school scheduled to be in the class at the selected time summed over all classes as taught
  • pupils engaged in private study at the selected time
  • pupils from this school who were part-time pupils not scheduled to be in attendance at the school at the selected time
  • pupils attending another school at the selected time

School characteristics

Childcare facilities indicator

Whether the pupil referral unit or alternative provision has childcare facilities.

Child mother indicator

Whether a pupil referral unit or alternative provision has provision for child mothers.

Departmental establishment number [used for funding]

The 4-digit reference number given to each school.

You can find it on get information about schools.

Governance

This shows your governance as indicated on your ‘Instrument of Governance’.

All academies use code ‘CA’.

Intake type

This shows your admissions policy, agreed with the local authority.

If you do not have a specific criteria for offering places then enter ‘COMP’ (comprehensive). This means comprehensive in the sense: ‘of broad scope’, which includes pupils living in a certain catchment area.

If you have more than one criteria for offering places, for example, if you are a catholic grammar, only enter one intake code.

Decide 1 main criteria and use this, even if all your criteria are equally weighted.

Local authority number [used for funding]

The 3-digit code that identifies a particular local authority. You can find yours in codeset CS003 in the common basic data set.

Maximum age – boys

The oldest boys can be to attend your school.

Maximum age – girls

The oldest girls can be to attend your school.

Maximum boarding pupils

The highest number of boarding pupils the school is approved to have.

Maximum day pupils

The highest number of day pupils the school is approved to have.

Maximum year group

The highest year group your school usually has provision for.

Special situations or pupils (for example ‘held back’) do not affect his.

Minimum age – boys

The youngest boys can be to attend your school.

Minimum age – girls

The youngest girls can be to attend your school.

Minimum year group

The lowest year group your school usually has provision for.

Special situations or pupils (for example ‘early entry’) do not affect his.

School email address

This should be:

  • the email you use for official communications
  • an email that the head can access
  • formatted as an email address, eg office@school.ac.uk

It should not be an individual’s email address.

The Department for Education may send emails to this address.

School name [used for funding]

In full, including the word ‘School’, ‘College’, ‘Academy’ or ‘Free School’ if part of the full name.

School phase [used for funding]

Make sure you enter the right phase. We ask for different information depending on your phase.

All-through academies

The code is ‘AS’

Do not use this code if your establishment number is:

  • 1000-1099
  • 1100-1150
  • 1800-1899
  • 5950-5999
  • 7000-7999

All other schools

Check your school phase code

School SEN type

The type of special need your school is approved to make provision for.

Check your school SEN type code.

School telephone number

The main number used for official purposes.

Use the full number, including the area code.

Special school organisation

Whether the school:

  • mainly provides accommodation for day pupils
  • mainly provides accommodation for boarding pupils
  • is a hospital school

Teenage mother places

How many places teenage mothers a pupil referral unit or alternative provision has.

Type of school [used for funding]

Use code 49 if you are an:

  • academy
  • alternative provision academy
  • alternative provision free school

Use code 54 if you are a local authority maintained pupil referral unit

Check your type of school code for all other types of school.

Unique reference number (URN)

The schools 6-digit unique reference number.

You can find it on get information about schools.

Survey reference date

The survey reference dates (formatted yyyy-mm-dd) are:

  • 2019-10-03 for the autumn census
  • 2020-01-16 for the spring census
  • 2020-05-21 for the summer census

These are automatically generated by your management information system, but you should still be able to change them is there are special circumstances.
